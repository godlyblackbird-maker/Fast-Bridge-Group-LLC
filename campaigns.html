SMS Launch Checklist

Build the operating system before anyone touches send.

  1. Clean the list: keep only active agents and brokers you genuinely want a relationship with. Remove duplicates, dead numbers, and anyone who already opted out.
  2. Register your traffic: complete A2P 10DLC brand and campaign registration before launch. Unregistered 10DLC traffic will bottleneck or get filtered.
  3. Segment the campaign: split by county, brokerage tier, listing side vs buyer-heavy agents, and recent transaction activity. One script should not hit every market.
  4. Personalize the first line: include agent first name, target geography, and buyer type. The first 60 characters decide whether the message survives.
  5. Set response ownership: every inbound needs a named human rep, response window, escalation path, and calendar-booking outcome.
  6. Track outcomes: log reply status, referral source, stale listing flags, pocket listing mentions, and whether the agent is worth future outreach.

Suggested Agent SMS Sequence

Short, respectful touches that feel like deal flow outreach rather than list spam.

Touch 1, Day 0: Ask for active/off-market inventory in a specific area and state your buying capability in one line.
Touch 2, Day 2: Follow up with a narrower ask, such as probate, fixer, stale listing, or failed escrow opportunities.
Touch 3, Day 5: Ask if they want your exact buy box and proof-of-funds ready list for faster matching.
Touch 4, Day 10: Close the loop politely and leave the door open for future pocket listings or problem sellers.

Go / No-Go Gates

Do not launch until every item below is true.

Ready Registered traffic: brand and use case approved for A2P 10DLC.
Ready Opt-out handling: STOP, UNSUBSCRIBE, and equivalent keywords suppress immediately.
Watch Inbox coverage: somebody owns inbound replies in real time during send windows.
Watch Volume ramp: start small by market, then scale only after reply and opt-out metrics stabilize.

Compliance Rules

Keep outreach useful, limited, and easy to exit.

  • Stay in local business hours and avoid weekends unless there is an active conversation.
  • Do not bury the sender identity. FAST BRIDGE GROUP should be recognizable in the message.
  • Keep first touches short. Long promotional texts get filtered and ignored.
  • Always include a simple opt-out path on campaign-style messages.
  • Pause any segment that trends toward complaints, blocks, or heavy opt-outs.

This is operational guidance, not legal advice. If you scale aggressively, have counsel review your workflow and copy.

Breakup / Keepalive
